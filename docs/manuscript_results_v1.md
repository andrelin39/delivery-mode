# Manuscript Results v1

## 3.1 Participant characteristics

The final analytic cohort included 1,364 births during the strict COVID-19 admission policy period. Labor onset was classified as spontaneous labor in 681 births and non-spontaneous labor or induction in 683 births. Sixteen records contained mixed or review-required labor onset information and were retained in the main analysis.

Maternal age was 31.28 plus or minus 4.81 years in the spontaneous labor group and 31.83 plus or minus 4.65 years in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group. Body mass index was 26.33 plus or minus 3.48 and 27.14 plus or minus 3.77, respectively. Gestational age was 38.79 plus or minus 0.85 weeks in the spontaneous labor group and 39.10 plus or minus 0.85 weeks in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group. Mean birth weight was 3007.14 plus or minus 331.67 g in the spontaneous labor group and 3129.07 plus or minus 332.89 g in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group. Median stage 1 labor duration was 580 minutes in the spontaneous labor group and 555 minutes in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group. Median stage 2 labor duration was 22 minutes and 24 minutes, respectively.

## 3.2 Mode of birth distribution

Mode of birth distribution differed by labor onset pathway. In the spontaneous labor group, normal spontaneous delivery was observed in 273 births (40.1%), vacuum extraction delivery in 388 births (57.0%), and cesarean delivery in 20 births (2.9%). In the non-spontaneous labor or induction group, normal spontaneous delivery was observed in 202 births (29.6%), vacuum extraction delivery in 403 births (59.0%), and cesarean delivery in 78 births (11.4%). No vaginal birth after cesarean cases were observed.

Operative delivery, defined as cesarean delivery or vacuum extraction delivery, was observed in 408 births (59.9%) in the spontaneous labor group and 481 births (70.4%) in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group.

## 3.3 Association between labor onset pathway and cesarean delivery

In the unadjusted logistic regression model, non-spontaneous labor or induction was associated with cesarean delivery compared with spontaneous labor, with an odds ratio of 4.26 (95% CI 2.58 to 7.05, p < 0.001). The model included 1,364 births and 98 cesarean delivery events, and convergence was achieved.

After adjustment for maternal age, body mass index, gestational age, birth weight, gravida, and para, non-spontaneous labor or induction remained significantly associated with cesarean delivery compared with spontaneous labor. The adjusted odds ratio was 4.24 (95% CI 2.47 to 7.27, p < 0.001). The adjusted model included 1,364 births and 98 cesarean delivery events, and convergence was achieved.

## 3.4 Multinomial analysis of mode of birth

The multinomial logistic regression model used normal spontaneous delivery as the reference outcome and included 1,364 births. The model converged. Compared with spontaneous labor, non-spontaneous labor or induction was associated with vacuum extraction delivery versus normal spontaneous delivery, with a relative risk ratio of 1.32 (95% CI 1.03 to 1.70, p = 0.026). Non-spontaneous labor or induction was also associated with cesarean delivery versus normal spontaneous delivery, with a relative risk ratio of 5.20 (95% CI 2.94 to 9.18, p < 0.001).

## 3.5 Secondary outcomes

Birth weight was higher in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group than in the spontaneous labor group, with means of 3129.07 plus or minus 332.89 g and 3007.14 plus or minus 331.67 g, respectively. Apgar score at 1 minute was 7.85 plus or minus 0.48 in the spontaneous labor group and 7.87 plus or minus 0.45 in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group. Apgar score at 5 minutes was 8.87 plus or minus 0.40 and 8.86 plus or minus 0.43, respectively.

Low Apgar candidate at 1 minute was observed in 11 births (1.6%) in each labor onset group. Low Apgar candidate at 5 minutes was observed in 2 births (0.3%) in the spontaneous labor group and 2 births (0.3%) in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group. Stage 1 labor duration had 20 missing values in the spontaneous labor group and 78 missing values in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group. Stage 2 labor duration had 21 missing values in the spontaneous labor group and 78 missing values in the non-spontaneous labor or induction group.

## 3.6 Sensitivity analysis

The sensitivity analysis excluded 16 records flagged as requiring review for labor onset classification. The adjusted logistic regression model included 1,348 births and 97 cesarean delivery events, and convergence was achieved. Non-spontaneous labor or induction remained significantly associated with cesarean delivery compared with spontaneous labor, with an adjusted odds ratio of 4.27 (95% CI 2.48 to 7.33, p < 0.001).
