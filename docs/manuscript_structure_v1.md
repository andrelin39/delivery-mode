# Manuscript Structure v1

## Title

Labor onset pathway and mode of birth during a strict COVID-19 admission policy period: a retrospective observational study

## Running title

Labor onset pathway and mode of birth

## Abstract structure

The abstract should follow the structure commonly used for Midwifery submissions: Objective, Design, Setting, Participants, Measurements and findings, and Key conclusions and implications for practice. The abstract should state that the strict COVID-19 admission policy period was the study context, not the exposure. The main finding should be framed as an association between non-spontaneous labor or induction and cesarean delivery, with the adjusted odds ratio and 95% confidence interval reported.

## Keywords

Labor onset; induction of labor; cesarean section; mode of birth; midwifery; COVID-19; retrospective study

## Methods

The Methods section should include Study design and setting, Participants, Labor onset pathway classification, Outcome measures, Covariates, Statistical analysis, and Sensitivity analysis. The current Methods v1 draft contains these sections and is suitable as a first full draft, pending local institutional details, ethics approval information, and any journal-specific reporting requirements.

## Results

The Results section should include Participant characteristics, Mode of birth distribution, Association between labor onset pathway and cesarean delivery, Multinomial analysis of mode of birth, Secondary outcomes, and Sensitivity analysis. The current Results v1 draft reports the analytic cohort, descriptive group differences, cesarean delivery logistic regression, multinomial mode-of-birth regression, secondary outcomes, and sensitivity analysis without causal interpretation.

## Discussion outline

The Discussion should open with a concise summary of the principal findings. It should then compare the findings with existing literature on induction or non-spontaneous labor onset and mode of birth. A separate paragraph should address clinical and midwifery implications, with careful wording that the study observed associations rather than causal effects. Another paragraph should discuss the strict COVID-19 admission policy period as the clinical context and avoid interpreting the policy as the exposure. The limitations paragraph should address retrospective design, single-institution data, possible residual confounding, classification uncertainty in mixed PGADM records, lack of observed VBAC cases, high VED frequency, and limited generalisability. The conclusion should state that non-spontaneous labor or induction was associated with cesarean delivery and mode-of-birth distribution during the study period.

## Items to strengthen before submission to Midwifery

The Introduction should be developed with current literature on induction of labor, non-spontaneous labor onset, cesarean delivery, operative birth, and midwifery-relevant implications. The Discussion should compare the observed associations with prior studies and explain relevance for maternity care without causal claims. Ethics approval, consent waiver status if applicable, institutional setting, inclusion and exclusion criteria, data period definition, and missing data handling should be described more explicitly. The statistical analysis section should specify software versions and any complete-case handling in the regression models. The high proportion of vacuum extraction delivery should be contextualised carefully. The absence of VBAC cases should be stated as a data feature and modeling limitation. Figure and table numbering should be checked against the final journal limits, and supplementary materials should be selected after confirming Midwifery formatting requirements.
